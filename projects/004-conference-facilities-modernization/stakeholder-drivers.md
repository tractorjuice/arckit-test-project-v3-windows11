## Executive Summary

### Purpose
This document identifies key stakeholders for the Conference Facilities Modernization project, their underlying drivers (motivations, concerns, needs), how these drivers manifest into SMART goals, and the measurable outcomes that will satisfy those goals. This analysis ensures stakeholder alignment and provides complete traceability from individual concerns to project success metrics.

### Key Findings

The Conference Facilities Modernization project has **12 distinct stakeholder groups** with **18 primary drivers** spanning FINANCIAL, OPERATIONAL, STRATEGIC, and USER/EXPERIENCE categories. Critical findings include:

- **Strong Financial Driver**: CFO demands 80% reduction in support costs (£120K annual savings) with 18-month ROI payback
- **Operational Crisis**: IT Operations overwhelmed with 200 AV support tickets/month - modernization is operational necessity, not luxury
- **Hybrid Work Imperative**: 60% of employees working hybrid schedules; current rooms fail remote participants causing inclusion issues
- **Executive Reputation Risk**: Current AV failures during client meetings damage brand and executive confidence
- **Conflicting Priorities**: Speed (6-month deployment for CIO) vs. Quality (acoustic perfection for Facilities Manager) requires compromise

**PRIMARY CONFLICT**: CFO's cost minimization (£750K budget cap) conflicts with Executive Assistants' demand for premium boardroom experience (£40K for one room). Resolution: Tiered room designs with 5 huddle rooms @ £8K funding 1 boardroom @ £30K (value-engineered from £40K).

### Critical Success Factors
- **ROI Achievement**: £251K annual net savings (£200K productivity + £120K support cost reduction - £69K OpEx) ≥ 18-month payback
- **Support Ticket Reduction**: 200/month → <40/month (80% reduction) within 6 months post-deployment
- **User Satisfaction**: Current 45% → Target >85% "satisfied" rating in quarterly surveys
- **Hybrid Meeting Quality**: >95% of remote participants rate audio/video as "good" or "excellent"
- **Budget Compliance**: Total CapEx ≤ £726,750 (£750K cap minus 3% buffer)

### Stakeholder Alignment Score
**Overall Alignment**: MEDIUM-HIGH (7/10)

**Alignment Analysis**:
- ✅ **Strong Alignment** (8 stakeholders): All agree modernization is necessary; support hybrid work enablement
- ⚠️ **Moderate Conflict** (2 conflicts): Speed vs Quality, Cost vs Premium Features (resolutions documented)
- ✅ **Executive Sponsorship**: CIO strongly backing project with strategic importance rating
- ⚠️ **Risk Area**: Acoustic quality concerns from Facilities Manager could delay deployment if not addressed in pilot

**Conflict Resolution Strategy**: Pilot phase (5 rooms, Week 1-4) includes 1 acoustically challenging room to validate audio quality before mass deployment. £30K contingency budget allocated for proactive acoustic panels in known problem rooms.

### SD-2: CFO - Cost Reduction & ROI Achievement

**Stakeholder**: Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

**Driver Category**: FINANCIAL

**Driver Statement**: Achieve demonstrable ROI within 18 months by reducing AV support costs by £120K/year and gaining productivity savings through elimination of meeting setup time waste. Budget must not exceed £750K CapEx cap.

**Context & Background**:
- **Financial Pressure**: Board demanding cost optimization across all departments
- **Current Waste**: 200 AV support tickets/month × 1.5 FTE = £60K/year labor waste

- **Accountability**: CFO must justify all investments to Board with clear ROI

**Driver Intensity**: **CRITICAL**

**Enablers**:

- Support cost reduction measurable: 200 tickets/month → <40/month (80% reduction)

- Tiered room budget approach maximizes value: £8K huddle rooms subsidize £30K boardroom

**Blockers**:
- Scope creep increasing costs (mitigation: Fixed scope, change control process)
- Premium feature requests from executives (mitigation: Tiered room designs, value engineering)
- Optimism bias in ROI calculations (mitigation: HM Treasury 15% adjustment factor applied)
- Delayed benefits realization if adoption slow (mitigation: Change management, forced adoption by retiring legacy projectors)
- Vendor price increases post-contract (mitigation: Fixed-price contract, 3-year warranty)

**Related Stakeholders**:
- **CIO** (SD-1): Shares strategic driver, provides executive sponsorship

## Driver-to-Goal Mapping

### Goal G-1: Reduce Meeting Setup Time by 80%

**Derived From Drivers**: SD-1 (CIO - Hybrid Work), SD-2 (CFO - Cost Reduction), SD-9 (End Users - Ease of Use)

**Goal Owner**: Facilities Manager

**Goal Statement**: Reduce average meeting setup time from 15-20 minutes to <3 minutes by implementing one-touch meeting join and wireless presentation, measured via room analytics, achieving target within 3 months post-deployment.

**Why This Matters**:
- **CFO Driver**: 15-20 min/meeting × 8,000 meetings/month = 2,000-2,667 hours/month waste = £80-100K/month labor cost
- **End User Driver**: Eliminates single biggest user pain point; directly improves daily work experience
- **CIO Driver**: Productivity gains demonstrate IT's strategic value and justify modernization investment

**Success Metrics**:
- **Primary Metric**: Average meeting start time (time from scheduled start to actual start)
  - **Baseline**: 15-20 minutes late average (user surveys)
  - **Target**: <3 minutes (95% of meetings start within 2 minutes of scheduled time)
  - **Measurement**: Room analytics timestamp data (meeting join events vs scheduled start time)

- **Secondary Metrics**:
  - AV support tickets for "meeting setup issues": 80/month → <10/month (87% reduction)
  - User satisfaction with "ease of use": 45% → >85% "satisfied" or "very satisfied"
  - Wireless presentation adoption rate: >60% of meetings use wireless vs HDMI cable

**Baseline**:
- Current: 15-20 minutes average meeting setup time (user survey, May 2025)
- Support tickets: 80/month classified as "setup issues" (projector won't work, can't find dongle, etc.)

  - **Savings Allocation**: £32K savings from huddle rooms fund £10K additional for boardroom vs standard

  - Executive Assistants accepted £30K boardroom vs £40K as "professional quality" (compromise: good enough for client meetings, not luxury)

  - **Physical controls**: USB ports on Teams Rooms compute device locked in AV rack; only table-facing USB-C port exposed

### Escalation Path

**Level 1: Day-to-Day Operations** (Facilities Manager / Project Manager)
- **Decisions**: Room deployment schedule, vendor coordination, user communication
- **Issues**: Minor delays (<1 week), user feedback, vendor questions
- **Response Time**: Same-day resolution
- **Example**: "Room 3A installation delayed 2 days due to network port issue" → Facilities Manager coordinates with IT Ops Network team to resolve

**Level 2: Steering Committee** (CIO, CFO, Facilities Manager, IT Ops Director, Procurement Manager)
- **Frequency**: Weekly 30-minute meeting (Mondays 9:00-9:30am)
- **Decisions**: Scope changes, budget variances (±5-10%), timeline adjustments, vendor escalations
- **Issues**: Support ticket trend concerning, acoustic quality concerns, user satisfaction below target
- **Response Time**: Within 1 week (steering committee meeting)
- **Example**: "Pilot phase acoustic testing shows echo in glass-walled room" → Steering Committee approves £15K acoustic panel treatment

**Level 3: Executive Escalation** (CIO → Board)
- **Decisions**: Project cancellation, major budget overruns (>10%), strategic direction changes, risks exceeding appetite
- **Issues**: ROI not achievable, stakeholder conflict unresolvable, regulatory compliance blocking deployment
- **Response Time**: Within 2 weeks (Board meeting schedule)
- **Example**: "CFO withdraws funding due to weak early ROI indicators" → CIO escalates to Board with risk-adjusted business case and mitigation plan

**Cross-Functional Escalations**:
- **Security/Privacy Issues** → Data Protection Officer → CIO → Board (if regulatory risk)
- **Vendor Performance Issues** → Procurement Manager → CFO → Legal (if contract dispute)
- **Network Infrastructure Blockers** → IT Operations Director → CIO (if impacting critical path)
